How To Care For Your Electric Bike

Keeping your electric bike in good working order improves your fun and safety. Electric bikes aren’t much more complicated than traditional bikes when it comes to maintenance - the key difference is the battery and electronic drive components. Most importantly, treat your battery with care, and always bring your electric bike in for professional service at least once a year. We’re happy to provide you with tips and advice on how to maintain your electric bike, so you can keep enjoying it!

How To Maintain Your Electric Bike

1

Charge Your Battery Properly

Each manufacturer provides appropriate charging equipment and specific instructions for proper and safe charging. MOST important, though, is never let a low or drained battery sit idle! Charge it to at least 60% for storage.

2

Keep Tires Properly Inflated

Underfilled tires can cause issues, especially when riding at high speeds. Make sure to keep a flat repair kit or a spare tube on you and know how to repair your flat.

3

Check Your Brakes

This applies to any type of bike, but with electric bikes, it’s especially important. You need to be able to brake comfortably, even at higher speeds even when the bike is heavier. 

4

Clean & Lube Your Chain

One of the most important parts of any bicycle, a well-maintained chain works with the drivetrain and allows you to shift gears. Regularly cleaning and lubing it is crucial! We recommend drip applications. Do not use a spray lubricant as over-spray can easily contaminate brake pads.

5

Schedule Regular Maintenance

If you’re riding your electric bike frequently, take it in for service at least once per year. This will extend your bike’s life and keep you riding longer. Remember, some maintenance now can save a lot in the long run. Plus, you love your bike, right? (If not then we can help with that, too.)
